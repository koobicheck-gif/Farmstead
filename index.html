The vision

A Christian event center for ministries needing retreats in Central Oklahoma.

Across Central Oklahoma, there are wonderful churches. Wonderful non-profits. Kingdom-minded ministries doing meaningful work. What they don't have is a dedicated, hospitality-grade place to gather. A place to send their pastors for renewal. A place to take their leadership team offsite. A place to host a marriage retreat, welcome major donors, or plan the next chapter of their mission.

The Farmstead is that place.

A historic homestead in Echontuchka valley.

A beautiful historic homestead, tucked into Echontuchka valley in central Oklahoma. An established wedding venue with real revenue and a real booking pipeline. Beautiful. Beloved. And ready to become something more.

How it works

Owned by Watersedge.Funded by your DAF.

The Farmstead Venue (two acres, the buildings, the venue business, and operations with equipment) will be owned by Watersedge, the foundation serving as nonprofit steward.

The acquisition is funded entirely through Donor Advised Fund grants from a founding circle of mission-aligned partners. At close, the property is debt-free, with the existing mortgage and land loan retired in full, and the family that built the Farmstead retains no ownership interest.

A board provides governance. An operator drawn from the current team runs the day-to-day. The mission is protected, in writing, in perpetuity.

The double impact

A double-impact opportunity for your DAF.

Most DAF grants do one good thing. This one does two.

First

Fund the acquisition

Your grant helps fund the acquisition, placing a Kingdom asset permanently into mission ownership. Held debt-free.

Then

The venue keeps giving

The venue continues to operate. Weddings continue. Retreats continue. The property generates surplus year after year. As surplus flows back to Watersedge, contributing donors are credited proportionally to their original grant, giving them capital to re-grant to ministries they love.

You are not just funding a building. You are building an engine, one that generates Kingdom impact directly and keeps sending capital back to you to redeploy year after year.

One acquisition. Perpetual impact. And a DAF that keeps giving through the years.

Built for the Kingdom. Funded by the Kingdom. Returned to the Kingdom.
